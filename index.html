Projects

TreeVisual Advisor: Professor Chaoli Wang. Collaborator: Brendan O'Handley.
TreeVisual is an educational software for teaching and learning tree visualization. It mainly uses JavaScript, d3.js, jQuery for frontend, and Ruby on Rails for backend. I implemented the quiz component, designed around 30 quiz questions, fixed several major bugs, and improved user experience.

Confronting Pandemic with Visual Insights: Collaborative Collection and Analysis of COVID-19 Visualizations Worldwide Advisor: Professor Xiaoru Yuan.
In this project, we built a platform of collecting, annotating and analyzing visualizations via crowdsourcing. We then collected more than 5,000 visualizations related to COVID-19. From them, we extracted some principles and guidlines of visually conveying COVID-related information.
I worked with peers on designing the platform (form layout, tooltips, etc.), and improved the backend by allowing users to upload multiple pictures; besides, I contributed to the dataset collection and annotation.

MakeUC 2021, Wolfram Award (top 15 out of 100). Oct. 9-10, 2021.

MLH Fellowship Prep Program. Nov. 8-26, 2021.
The MLH Fellowship Prep Program is a 3-week intense online program for programmers around the world (especially from underrepresented groups) to experience collaborating, experimenting new technologies, and building projects through a short hackathon sprint. In this Nov. prep program, I used Ruby and Jekyll to build a team website, and used React to build a weather prediction website with a trip planner.
